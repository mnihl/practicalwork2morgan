Fundamentals of Computer Engineering (FCE)

Technical Information:

  • Grade: Computer Science
  • Field of Knowledge: Engineering and Architecture
  • School: Higher Polytechnic School
  • Subject: Fundamentals of Computer Engineering
  • Type: Basic Training
  • ECTS Credits: 6
  • Year: First
  • Code: 5614
  • Teaching Period: First semester

Objectives:

The main objective of the "Fundamentals of Computer Engineering" course is to provide a comprehensive understanding of Computer Engineering from a global perspective, encompassing both academic and professional dimensions. This course is designed to equip students with foundational knowledge rooted in disciplines such as mathematics, physics, anthropology, and ethics. Additionally, it aims to elucidate prospective career trajectories in the field and elucidate the pivotal role played by computer engineers in contemporary society

Learning Outcomes:

  • Students must have demonstrated knowledge and understanding in an area of study that is founded on general secondary education. Moreover, the area of study is typically at a level that includes certain aspects implying knowledge at the forefront of its field of study, albeit supported by advanced textbooks.
  • Students must be able to apply their knowledge to their work or vocation in a professional manner and possess skills that can typically be demonstrated by coming up with and sustaining arguments and solving problems within their field of study.
  • Students must have the ability to gather and interpret relevant data (usually within their field of study) in order to make judgments that include reflections on pertinent social, scientific or ethical issues.
  • Students must be able to convey information, ideas, problems and solutions to both an expert and non-expert audience.
  • Students must have developed the learning skills needed to undertake further study with a high degree of independence.
